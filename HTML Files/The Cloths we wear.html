In India, the climate condition is not the same throughout the year. India that has places are cold and places that are hot. In cold places, people wear woollen clothes. People wear cotton clothes in hot places. So, the climate of a place determines the type of clothes people wear.

There are some clothes that are common and popular throughout India. Sarees salwar-kameez, trousers, shirts, skirts, churidar pyjamas-kurta and jeans are some such clothes.

DRESSES OF MEN

The traditional dresses of men are dhoti-kurta in North and East India. Some men wear pyjama-kurta and lungi-kurta also. Churidar pyjamas, achkans and jackets are also used by men.

In Tamil Nadu and Kerala, the traditional dress for men is a lungi with a shirt or kurta. In Tamil Nadu, Men's lungi is called veshti and in Kerala it is called mundu.

Children, you will notice that most of the men living around you wear trousers and shirts. In modern days, the use of trousers and shirts is increasing in almost all parts of the country. The reason is that they are simple to make and convenient to wear especially at workplaces.

Some other modern dresses for men are safari suits, jeans, T-shirts, shorts, etc. Some men wear woollen coats and trousers with neckties in winter.

People use different kinds of headgear in different parts of our country. In Uttar Pradesh (UP), Haryana, Gujarat and Maharashtra, the use of 'Gandhi Cap' is common. The use of a turban is common in Punjab. The colourful round 'Kullucap' of Himachal Pradesh is also very popular.

DRESSES OF WOMEN

The most popular dress of women in our country is the 'saree-blouse'. It is worn by women in almost all states of our country. But they are worn in different styles in different areas. If you look at the styles of sarees worn by women of different areas, you can notice the difference.

Another very common dress which is worn by women of many states especially in UP, Punjab and Haryana is the salwar-kameez with dupatta. Some women also wear ghagra-kurta with odhni.

Ghaghra-katchli and lehnga-choli are more commonly worn by women in the villages of Rajasthan and Gujarat. In Kerala, women wear mundu-blouse.

In Kashmir and Ladakh, men and women both wear a long woollen 'kurta' called 'Phiran'. They sometimes cover their bodies with blankets to protect themselves from very cold weather.

The tribal people of our country have their own tribal styles of dresses.

UNIFORMS

People who do special jobs wear uniforms. Soldiers wear tough clothes. Policemen wear khaki uniforms. Doctors and nurses wear white uniforms. School children also wear uniforms at school. On special occasions like wedding and festivals, people wear colourful or fancy dresses.
